## 📝 About Ethiopian Cuisine

### 🍞 Injera
All stews and dishes are traditionally served with **injera** - a spongy, sourdough flatbread made from teff flour. It serves as both plate and utensil!

### 🌶️ Berbere
The signature spice blend of Ethiopian cuisine, made with chili peppers, garlic, ginger, and various aromatic spices.

### 🥄 Eating Style
Ethiopian food is traditionally eaten with your hands, using pieces of injera to scoop up the stews and vegetables.

### 🍽️ Sharing Culture
Ethiopian meals are often served on a large communal platter, encouraging sharing and togetherness.

## ☕ Coffee Ceremony

### Traditional Ethiopian Coffee (ቡና)
Experience the traditional Ethiopian coffee ceremony:
1. Fresh roasting of green coffee beans
2. Grinding by hand
3. Brewing in traditional jebena (clay pot)
4. Served in three rounds: Abol, Tona, Baraka

**Price:** 50 Birr per person
**Time:** 30-45 minutes
**Reservation required for groups**
